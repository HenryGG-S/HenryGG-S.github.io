This group project produced a mobile-responsive internal application for the University of Exeter Medical School, designed to reduce reliance on ad hoc manual notification for time-sensitive lab deliveries.

I owned the porter-facing workflow, translating operational needs into software behaviour including purchase-order lookup, researcher and team matching, delivery-location guidance, and staged email notifications. I also contributed to technical planning, architecture discussions, and handover documentation so the system could be adopted and maintained after the project ended.

The project served approximately 150 research staff across around 25 lab teams, with GDPR and security considerations in scope. It was a useful exercise in building software around real users, imperfect requirements, maintainability, and practical operational constraints.
